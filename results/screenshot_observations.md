# Mountain Screenshot Observations

## 6-Panel Conditions (mountain_screenshots.png)

1. **Exp4 Baseline (29.4% summit)**: Particles cluster on the two local peaks (red triangles) in the lower-left region. A few scattered near the summit (gold star, upper right). Most trapped on local optima.

2. **Exp4 All Visionaries (100% summit)**: All 500 particles converged to the global summit. The visionary signal completely dominates — everyone marches to the peak. This is the "too many visionaries" problem — it works but at what cost?

3. **Exp5 Specialist coupling=1.0 (0.2% summit)**: Almost all particles trapped on local peaks. Strategy is locked to preferences, so social conformity in pref space = strategic conformity. Particles are tightly clustered (similar colors) on the wrong peaks.

4. **Exp5 Generalist coupling=0.0 (30.2% summit)**: Particles spread across the landscape with diverse colors (diverse team identities). Some found the summit, many still exploring. The decoupling allows strategic exploration independent of team identity.

5. **Exp5 Rare Vis + Generalist (33.8% summit)**: Best dual-space condition. Similar spread to generalist but slightly more particles near the summit, pulled by the 5% visionaries.

6. **Exp5 Specialist + Differentiation (13.2% summit)**: Particles scattered widely across the landscape with very diverse colors. Social differentiation prevents clustering (0% trapped) but also prevents convergence on the summit. The scatter is visible — particles everywhere, not concentrated anywhere.

## Landscape Overview (mountain_landscape_overview.png)

- **Fitness landscape**: Three peaks visible — global summit (gold star, upper right, tallest), two local peaks (red triangles, lower left, shorter). The landscape has clear gradient structure.
- **Cost terrain**: A single cost ridge centered around (0.35, 0.35). The direct path from the center of the space toward the summit crosses this expensive region. Green (cheap) surrounds it.
- **Best condition overlay**: Particles from the Rare Vis + Generalist condition shown on the mountain. Most are in the low-fitness flat region, with a cluster near the summit.
